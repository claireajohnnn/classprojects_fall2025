How the shine gets on stage

Costume, sound & light

The sparkle starts in the costume shop where silvers, chromes, and rhinestones are cut to move under light. Fabrics are tested against red gels and cool ambers to avoid color cast that dulls the gowns. Hidden snaps and rigging enable quick-changes that flip eras between musical cues without breaking flow. Microphone placement balances choreography with breath so vocals stay warm and present. Monitor mixes are tuned per venue so the trio can lock harmonies over the band without strain. Every choice aims for clarity: the audience sees the shimmer and hears the truth on the same beat.

Lighting plots map reveals, blackouts, and mirror hits that sculpt the chrome look of the show. Stage managers call dozens of rail, deck, and light cues so transitions feel like live music TV. Props teams track jewelry and accessories that read from the back row without catching on fabric. Orchestrations ride the rhythm section while brass punches through like camera flashes. On two-show days, crews reset stones, restitch hems, and swap footwear to keep everyone safe and sharp. When it works, the polish feels effortless even though it’s a thousand tiny routines.
